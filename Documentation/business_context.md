## Industry Context
Financial services organizations manage large portfolios of customers with varying credit behaviors. 
Customer delinquency poses a significant risk to revenue, regulatory compliance, and long-term customer relationships.

With increasing data availability, financial institutions are exploring AI-driven and analytics-based approaches 
to proactively identify customers at risk of delinquency and intervene early.

---

## Business Problem
The client is facing challenges in:
- Identifying early warning signals of customer delinquency
- Prioritizing customers for intervention efficiently
- Reducing manual effort in collections while maintaining ethical practices
- Ensuring compliance with financial regulations and responsible AI guidelines

Traditional rule-based approaches lack scalability and adaptability, 
leading to delayed actions and suboptimal customer outcomes.

---

## Project Objective
The objective of this job simulation was to:
- Analyze customer-related data to uncover potential delinquency risk indicators
- Structure insights through exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Design a no-code predictive framework to categorize customer risk levels
- Propose an AI-powered collections strategy aligned with ethical and regulatory standards

---

## Scope of the Simulation
- Focused on **conceptual analytics and strategy design**
- No direct model training or production deployment
- Emphasis on **decision-making, logic structuring, and business impact**

This approach mirrors early-stage AI consulting engagements 
where problem framing and solution design are critical.
